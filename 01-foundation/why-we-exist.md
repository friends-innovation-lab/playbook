# Why Friends Innovation Lab Exists

*Internal North Star Document - Last updated: January 12, 2025*

---

## The Problem We Saw

There's a gap in the market that nobody's serving well.

**For small businesses:** You're stuck between "figure it out yourself" and "pay $500/month for a SaaS product that only does 30% of what you need." You end up with duct-taped spreadsheets, tools that don't match your workflow, or software you're paying for but barely using. You know there's a better way, but custom development has always been out of reach.

**For government agencies:** You spend millions on contracts based on requirements documents that are educated guesses at best. By the time you realize the thing you bought doesn't work, you've burned years and budgets. Everyone knows the system is broken, but the alternative - building something to test the idea first - has never been fast or cheap enough to be practical.

Both sides have the same root problem: **you can't afford to experiment.**

---

## What Changed

AI-augmented development changed the economics.

Over the past year, working with tools like Claude Code, I've discovered something: what used to take weeks now takes days. What used to require a team now requires one focused founder. The cost of building a working prototype has dropped dramatically - but most people don't have access to that capability yet.

Friends Innovation Lab is how we share that capability.

## What We Actually Do

We build custom prototypes - fast, affordable, and right-sized for the problem.

**For small businesses:** Internal tools that match exactly how you work. Not a generic CRM - *your* CRM. Not an off-the-shelf scheduler - a scheduling tool built around *your* constraints. Custom software at a price point that used to be impossible.

**For government agencies:** Pre-procurement prototypes. Before you write the RFP, before you commit millions, let us build a working version in 6-8 weeks. Test it with real users. See what actually works. Then write requirements based on evidence, not assumptions.

## The Bigger Vision

Friends Innovation Lab isn't just a service line. It's an engine.

**Revenue stream 1: Client services.** Paid prototype engagements for small businesses and government. Profitable on their own.

**Revenue stream 2: Contract feeder.** A prototype for a government agency can become a full build-out contract for Friends From The City. The lab de-risks the sale.

**Revenue stream 3: Product incubator.** Some prototypes reveal product opportunities. When that happens, we can spin them into standalone products that Friends owns. TrueBid is the first example - a prototype that's becoming a SaaS product with its own LLC.

One capability. Three paths to revenue. That's the model.
